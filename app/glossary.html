Probability Density Function#

A probability density function over some space is a function that takes value in \(\mathbb{R}^+\) and whose integral over that space is one:

\[ p: \Omega \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^+ ~~\text{s.t.}~~ \int_\Omega p(x) \mathrm{d}x = 1 \]
CDF#
Cumulative Distribution Function#

Only defined for univariate distributions. It is the probability that a random variable takes any value below a given one:

\[\begin{split} F: \left\{\begin{array}{rcl} \Omega & \rightarrow & [0, 1] \\ x & \mapsto & \int_{-\infty}^x p(z) \mathrm{d}z \end{array}\right. \end{split}\]

Likelihood#

In Bayesian theory, a probability distribution, \(p(\mathbf{X}\mid\mathbf{Z})\), defined over some measurement \(\mathbf{X}\), conditioned on some unobserved quantity \(\mathbf{Z}\)`.

Can also more generally relate to any analytical probability density. For example, the joint likelihood of a model is the probability density defined over the union of all its variables.

Prior#

In Bayesian theory, a probability distribution, \(p(\mathbf{Z})\), defined over some unobserved quantity \(\mathbf{Z}\), that does not depend on any measurement.

Posterior#

In Bayesian theory, a probability distribution, \(p(\mathbf{Z}\mid\mathbf{X})\), defined over some unobserved quantity \(\mathbf{Z}\), conditioned on a measurement \(\mathbf{X}\).
